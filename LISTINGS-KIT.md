  these are **parked** (bottom section).

  skip the phone field or park that channel — **never publish a fake number** and **never
  fabricate an address / use a PO Box**; that's what gets listings banned.

# 🔗 Foundation Backlink Layer (indexation — address-free)

**Different goal from above.** The listings kit builds brand presence; this layer gets
Google to **discover, crawl, and index** the site's pages — especially the slow-to-index
location pages on a new low-authority domain. This directly attacks
*"Discovered – currently not indexed,"* the portfolio's #1 bottleneck.

### Honest expectations
- Most free Web 2.0 links are **nofollow** (Medium, LinkedIn, Pinterest) — but Google
  still **crawls** them, so it follows the link to *discover* your pages. For a new site,
  crawl-discovery is the real win, plus referral traffic + brand mention.
- **dofollow (real equity):** a self-hosted **Tumblr** or **WordPress.com** post body link.
- **Quality > quantity.** 5–8 links from real, already-indexed properties beat 50
  auto-submitted directory links (a footprint risk). **No directory-blast tools.**
- All address-free, owned by the backstage "John Ranks" persona; brand = Central Ohio Pressure Washing.
- **No PBN cross-linking** — do NOT link the other 4 sites to this one (5-site interlink = footprint).

### Draft — Medium article (publish under "Central Ohio Pressure Washing")
**Title:** Soft Washing vs. Pressure Washing: How to Clean a Columbus Home Without Wrecking It

> Every Central Ohio spring, homeowners notice the same things: green algae creeping up the
> north-facing siding, black streaks running down the roof, and a driveway that's gone gray
> with grime. The instinct is to rent a pressure washer and blast it all off. For siding and
> roofs, that's exactly the wrong move — and it's the most expensive mistake in exterior
> cleaning.
>
> **Why high pressure damages siding and roofs.** A pressure washer can force water *behind*
> vinyl and lap siding, strip paint, and — on a shingle roof — blast off the protective
> granules that make shingles last. Asphalt shingles and most siding are not meant to take
> 3,000 PSI. Here's the
> [full soft-wash vs. pressure-wash breakdown](https://centralohiopressurewashing.com/soft-washing-vs-pressure-washing-columbus/).
>
> **What soft washing actually is.** Soft washing uses **low pressure** plus a cleaning
> solution that kills the algae, mold, and mildew *at the root*, then rinses clean. The growth
> doesn't just look gone — it's actually dead, so it stays away far longer than a quick blast.
> It's the correct method for siding, roofs, and painted/wood surfaces.
>
> **When pressure *is* right.** Hard, flat surfaces — concrete driveways, sidewalks, paver
> patios — can take controlled pressure to lift oil, grime, and freeze-thaw staining.
> Match the method to the surface and everything gets clean without damage.
>
> **How often?** Most Central Ohio homes do well with a soft wash every 1–2 years; heavily
> shaded or wooded lots (think Powell, Lewis Center) may want it annually. More on
> [how often to soft-wash](https://centralohiopressurewashing.com/how-often-should-you-soft-wash-your-house/),
> and [the best time of year to do it](https://centralohiopressurewashing.com/best-time-to-pressure-wash-house-ohio/).
>
> **Bottom line.** Soft wash the soft stuff (siding, roof), pressure wash the hard stuff
> (concrete) — and never the reverse. If you'd rather not risk it, [Central Ohio Pressure
> Washing](https://centralohiopressurewashing.com/) cleans home exteriors safely across the
> Columbus metro — free written estimates.
